**Two adversarial passes, both incorporated:**
- *Pass 1* caught two product-killing flaws — wash-trading 10× $0.001 sales scored SAFE; parked domains scored "live". Fixed + regression-tested.
- *Pass 2* (red-team on the hardened code) proved each fix passed its own test but fell to a variation, and found **4 real holes**: a **decoy** service inflating the median fee re-opened the cent wash-trade; `verify_envelope` trusted the envelope's own pubkey (**forgery**); `expires_at` was unsigned (**replay**); free services bypassed the volume gate. All four fixed (min-non-zero-fee + count floor, pinned pubkey, expiry recomputed from signed fields) and locked with regressions. Re-verified live: Otto/Explorer stayed SAFE, every attack blocked.

This is the real signal that the trust logic is defensible — it survived being attacked twice and the fixes were checked against live data each time.

## Reverted

- **First liveness hardening over-corrected** — treating only `2xx` as healthy flipped legit high-sales agents (Otto, Data Explorer) to CAUTION, because A2MCP endpoints are POST-first and answer GET with 402/405. Replaced with a behaviour-aware probe (POST+GET, JSON/402 = serving) after empirically checking what real endpoints return.
- **Sub-cent hard-cap** — first volume rule would have wrongly CAUTION'd Otto (169 real sales, cheap calls). Reworked so a high *count* (costly to wash) also clears SAFE, not just high per-call price.
- **Name-resolution `items[0]` fallback** — returned a confidently *wrong* agent ("Factor Credit Desk" for "Otto AI"). Changed to exact-match-or-None.
- **`median(fee)` volume proxy** — a decoy service inflated it. Replaced with **min non-zero fee** + a count floor for sub-cent pricing.

### Known residual limits (logged, not yet closed)
- **Sub-cent wash at scale:** a 100-count of $0.001 sales (~$0.10 + 100 gas txs + likely sock-puppet buyers) can still reach SAFE. The true fix needs a **distinct-buyer count** or on-chain settlement history — OKX exposes `buyerCount` in search but it's `null` in current data. Raised the bar from $0.01→~$0.10+effort; not eliminated.
- **Static-`{}` liveness:** a 200 JSON stub on a parked host still classifies as "serving". Mitigated by the volume gate (liveness alone can't produce SAFE), not by the probe. A real x402/A2MCP protocol handshake would close it.
- **Per-service scoring:** the verdict scores the agent, not the specific service the caller will invoke (a live agent with one dead service among many).
